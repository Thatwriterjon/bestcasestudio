# Case Studies: Proof Through Transformation

## What Is a Case Study?

A case study is a detailed narrative showing a specific customer's journey with your product. It includes:

- **The situation:** Who the customer was and what problem they faced
- **The approach:** How they used your product
- **The results:** What changed, with specific metrics
- **The testimony:** A quote from the customer

A good case study proves: "Real company faced real problem. Used our product. Got real results."

## Why Case Studies Are Your Strongest Social Proof

Case studies outperform all other social proof because they:

1. **Show process, not just promises.** You describe how the customer actually used your product.
2. **Prove ROI, not just claims.** You show specific metrics and results.
3. **Feel real, not marketing.** A narrative is harder to dismiss as spin than a quote.
4. **Build trust through specificity.** The more details, the more credible it feels.

A prospect reading a case study thinks: "This company solved this problem for a company like mine. They can solve it for us."

## Video vs. Written Case Studies

**Video case studies:** A customer on camera talking about their results. 90 seconds to 5 minutes.

**Written case studies:** A detailed narrative of the customer's journey and results. 800-1500 words.

**The data:**
- Video case studies have 40-60% higher completion rates
- Video case studies generate 15-25% conversion rates from viewers
- Written case studies have deeper information but lower engagement

**Recommendation:** Use video case studies for top-of-funnel awareness and conversion. Use written case studies for detailed, technical evaluation.

## How to Get Customers to Do Case Studies

1. **Ask your best customers.** The ones with the best results and happiest with your product.
2. **Make it easy.** Offer to do the interview, write the whole thing, handle all logistics.
3. **Compensate if needed.** $500-1000 is reasonable if they're reluctant.
4. **Let them approve.** Show them the draft, get their sign-off before publishing.
5. **Make it valuable to them.** Position it as a win for their visibility, not just yours.

Most good customers will say yes if you ask nicely.

## Structure of a Converting Case Study

**Section 1: The Situation** (Who they are and what they faced)

Introduce the customer. Be specific about their company, their size, their market, and their problem.

Don't say: "Company X needed better tools."

Say: "Company X was a $2M ARR SaaS platform targeting mid-market operations teams. Their product solved a real problem, but sales conversations were long and deals were hard to close."

**Section 2: The Challenge** (The specific problem)

Describe the exact problem they faced with nuance and specificity.

Don't say: "They had low sales conversion."

Say: "Prospects understood the product worked, but couldn't articulate when it was the answer versus building automation in-house. This confusion extended sales cycles from 45 days to 90+. CAC hit $8,000."

**Section 3: The Approach** (How they used your product)

Describe the method they used. Be specific about the steps, the effort, the timeline.

Don't say: "We helped them with positioning."

Say: "They identified three things: (1) their ideal customer profile, (2) the specific problem they solved better than anyone, (3) how to communicate that in a single sentence. They tested this positioning with 10 prospects before going live."

**Section 4: The Results** (What changed)

Show specific metrics with context. Include timeline.

Don't say: "Revenue increased."

Say: "Within 4 months: Sales cycle dropped from 90 to 40 days. CAC fell from $8,000 to $3,500. Closing rate went from 40% to 65%. They closed four six-figure deals they would have lost."

**Section 5: The Testimony** (Their words)

End with a specific quote from the customer.

Don't say: "Great to work with."

Say: "Their CEO: 'We stopped explaining ourselves and started being ourselves. Prospects got it faster. Closing became easier. This changed everything.'"

## Where to Publish Case Studies

1. **Your website** — Dedicated case studies page
2. **LinkedIn** — Share key metrics and learnings
3. **Email sequences** — Send relevant case studies to prospects
4. **Ads** — Use metrics and quotes in ad creative
5. **Sales materials** — Send to prospects during sales conversations

One case study becomes five pieces of content.

## Key Takeaway

Case studies prove that your product works for real companies facing real problems.

The more specific and detailed your case study, the more credible it is.

Start with one case study from your best customer. Measure the impact. Build from there.

That's how SaaS companies actually prove their product works.
